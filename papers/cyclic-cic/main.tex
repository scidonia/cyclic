\begin{abstract}
This note outlines a dependent type theory close to the Calculus of Inductive Constructions (CIC), together with a cyclic proof language whose proof objects are finite graphs.
The motivating idea is to represent recursion and extensional reasoning using cycles (plus a global progress condition), rather than storing general recursion syntax (\texttt{fix}) inside proof objects.
We also summarize term/program transformations (\textsc{CaseCase}, head \(\beta\)-reduction, and fold/refold of cycles) and explain why global cycle conditions are the key to their semantic viability.
\end{abstract}
